ASIA — EAST ASIA

JAPAN — Moderate, $185k, Active

Japan has NO retirement, passive-income, or investor-residency visa. The Business Manager visa was drastically tightened effective October 16, 2025: ¥30M (~$200,000) minimum capital (was ¥5M); at least one full-time employee who is a Japanese national/PR/settled-status holder; 3+ years of management experience OR a relevant master's; Japanese proficiency (applicant or the employee — N2 cited); no home offices; expert-vetted business plan. Existing holders have until October 16, 2028. Alternatives: Startup Visa (municipal endorsement) or HSP/J-Skip points routes.

13/07/2026 Update: Spoke with two immigration lawyers. They said that it is possible that I can have a successful application if I invest this money into a startup looking for that amount of capital (30 million yen) and then work in management activities at the company while appointed in a director position. The fact that I may need to be a registered director of the company may make this difficult, as the owners would have to introduce someone they don't know as an additional owner of the company. However, this could possibly be structured in a way such that we form a joint venture that is a subsidiary under the parent company, and maybe I could satisfy the commercial office lease, 1 full-time hire, and other requirements from the subsidiary's affiliation with the parent company. I would then only be a director of the subsidiary and the parent would be shielded from this new outsider (me) being a risk to the health and operations of the company. However, knowing Japanese people, this may be very difficult. They are extremely simplistic when it comes to business and very risk-averse. The mere idea of a foreign person changing their company structure could be something they don't want to deal with just from first glance. Companies which are starving for capital or are in financial trouble may be more open to this kind of arrangement.

PR: Available — standard PR takes 10 years of residence (3 on work visas), BUT the Highly Skilled Professional points system cuts this to 3 years, or just 1 year at 80+ points — the realistic fast lane if your income/degrees/Japanese score well. Citizenship: Available after 5 years of residence (naturalization can actually be faster than PR); Japan requires renouncing prior citizenship, which is the desired outcome. Real barrier is Japanese-language proficiency and Ministry of Justice discretion.

SOUTH KOREA — Moderate, $75k, Active

Two real routes, both with tradeoffs. D-8 Corporate Investment (active business route): minimum KRW 100 million (~USD 75,000) into a Korean corporation you control (≥10% voting shares). Source of funds must originate from your personal overseas account. For investments under KRW 300M immigration conducts a physical site inspection to verify office and workspace exist; at renewal, immigration wants to see actual revenue, tax filings, and operational records — not just a capital deposit sitting untouched. Consular scrutiny has tightened in 2026. This is an active-operating-business route, same category as the Poland/Estonia/Lithuania ones that were already excluded. F-2-12 IISPB (passive fund route, over cap): KRW 1.5 billion (~USD 1.15M) deposited into a Korea Development Bank-managed public interest fund for 5 years, principal returned without interest. KRW 3 billion (~USD 2.3M) tier grants immediate F-5 PR. Both IISPB tiers are far over cap. Real-estate F-2-8 (also over cap): KRW 1 billion (~USD 780k) in designated tourism/leisure zones (Jeju, Alpensia, Incheon FEZ, Haeundae); program scheduled to sunset April 30, 2026 unless extended.

PR: F-5 attainable via 5 years on D-8 plus points-based factors (or immediately via the KRW 3B IISPB tier, over cap); F-5-5 direct PR requires personal USD 500k investment under FIPA + employment of 5 Koreans, also over cap. Citizenship: Available after 5 years F-5 residence plus TOPIK Level 3 Korean language. Korea generally requires renunciation of prior citizenship (the goal). Language and Ministry of Justice discretion are the real barriers.

TAIWAN — Hard, $200k, Active

Investor Resident Visa: NT$6,000,000 (~$200,000) into a Taiwanese company, approved by the MOEA Investment Commission → resident visa + ARC for you and dependents. Entrepreneur Visa alternative: NT$500,000 (~$16,000) to start, renewal needs ~NT$3M turnover by year two or funding/incubator/patent qualification. No passive-income visa. Foreign-source income largely untaxed below high thresholds.

PR: Available — APRC (permanent residency) after 5 years of residence at 183+ days/year while maintaining the investment. Citizenship: Available after 5 years; naturalization requires renouncing prior citizenship (the goal); language and residency proofs are the real gates.

ASIA — SOUTHEAST ASIA

INDONESIA — Hard, ~$620k, Active

Investor KITAS (E28A, Index 313/314) requires IDR 10 billion (~$620k at current FX) in personal paid-up shareholding in an active PT PMA (Indonesian foreign investment company), plus a strategic management role (Director or Commissioner) filed with Menkumham. The two-year Investor KITAS is renewable; after 3–5 consecutive years of unbroken KITAS residence the holder can convert to Investor KITAP inside Indonesia (status conversion, not offshore). Best-practice guidance for 2026 is to budget 4–5 years on KITAS before conversion is granted. IMTA work-permit fee waived for Investor KITAS holders. Passive alternatives exist but are dead-ends for PR: E28C Golden Visa (from $350k in government bonds / listed equities / deposits) and the Second Home Visa (~$130k / IDR 2B proof-of-funds) both grant 5- or 10-year renewable permits that do not lead to KITAP. New in 2025: Global Citizenship of Indonesia (GCI) is diaspora-only, not applicable.

PR: Available via KITAP after 3–5 years on Investor KITAS. KITAP is a 5-year renewable permit — renewable indefinitely as long as the underlying PT PMA remains compliant and the shareholding is maintained. Not unconditional PR in the Western sense: rules can change at each renewal, and residency is tied to PMA standing (if the company loses standing, KITAP is at risk). Grants e-KTP Orang Asing (5-yr foreign national ID), no MERP required for re-entry, own property under Hak Pakai, broader banking/financial credibility. Citizenship: Eligible after two KITAP renewal cycles (~10+ years total residence post-KITAP grant, or roughly 13–15 years from first KITAS). Indonesia generally requires renunciation of prior citizenship — the desired outcome. Bahasa Indonesia proficiency, clean tax record, and cultural integration proofs are the real gates.

EUROPE — NORTHERN

LATVIA — Easy, €280k, Passive

Currently: €280,000 into a 5-year subordinated bank deposit (~3–4% yield) plus ~€30,000 in fees (€25,000 non-refundable). 5-year permit, no stay requirement. Parliament has been moving to a €150,000 State Alternative Investment Fund option with a €10,000 fee (smart energy / robotics / municipal debt — return uncertain). (A €50k company route exists but requires €40,000/YEAR in taxes paid — ~€200k over 5 years; economically worthless, ignore it.) The older €250,000 real-estate route (+5% fee) still exists on paper but is suspended for RU/BY nationals and administratively disfavored.

PR: Available after 5 years — but for Latvian PR (and the separate EU long-term status) you must have actually been residing there, unlike the visa itself which has no stay requirement. Citizenship: Available roughly 10 years in (5 on permits + 5 as PR) with Latvian language; the US is on Latvia's dual-citizenship whitelist so renunciation isn't forced (you can renounce independently regardless).

EUROPE — EASTERN

HUNGARY — Easy, €250k, Passive

€250,000 (~$270,000) into a National Bank-regulated real-estate fund (≥40% residential; 5-year lock; two approved funds: SPRINT, Gravitas) → 10-YEAR permit, renewable once for 10 more. No stay requirement; funds transfer only AFTER visa approval; €1M donation alternative; €500k direct-property option abolished Jan 2025; RU/BY excluded; work rights included; investment recoverable after the lock.

PR: Available — the 10+10-year permit is not PR, but actual permanent residency requires living in Hungary ~183 days/year for 3 years (guest investors are exempt from the new cultural test). Citizenship: Available after 8 years of continuous residence (≤45 days/year abroad) with Hungarian language — and Hungarian is the barrier; dual citizenship allowed.

ROMANIA — Moderate, $59k, Active

No passive-income or property visa. The standard shareholder route (D/AC visa) is genuinely hard: a ministry-endorsed business plan, ~$118k (€100k) shown in a Romanian bank, the investment executed within 12 months, and 10 full-time jobs created within 12 months (SRL; 15 for an SA). Three findings soften this dramatically. (1) THE ADMINISTRATOR ROUTE: be appointed administrator of a Romanian company that has received a ~$59k (€50k) capital or technology contribution → 1-year renewable permit; renewal requires only that the €50k remains invested, and a building purchased for the company's use explicitly qualifies — rental real estate can literally be the qualifying investment. Needs ~$590/mo (€500) income from outside the Romanian company; one non-EU administrator per company; you must not have held any Romanian company role in the prior 2 years. (2) THE US-CITIZEN EXEMPTION: Romanian immigration law firms state that citizens of the US, Canada, Japan, and Switzerland are exempt under bilateral treaties from the investment and job-creation requirements — basic requirements only (company role, accommodation, insurance, means of support) [verify exact scope with IGI before filing; three sources including a practicing Romanian firm, statutory paragraph not sighted directly]. (3) A ~$470k (€400k) golden visa (bonds / property held 5 yrs / shares, 5-year permit) was drafted October 2025 — not yet law. Extension mechanics (official IGI): 1 year at a time; means-of-support ~$590/mo (associates) or ~$825/mo (€700, shareholders); ~$175k (€150k) invested or 25 jobs relaxes the means test, ~$235k (€200k) or 50 jobs removes it; ~$590k (€500k) or 50+ jobs → 3-year permits. Taxes: 10% flat personal income tax; micro-company regime taxes turnover at 1–3% up to ~$1.17M (€1M).

PR: Available — 5 years of continuous temporary residence (an investment of ~$1.17M (€1M) or 100+ jobs waives the duration and subsistence conditions entirely). Citizenship: 8 years standard (5 if married to a Romanian citizen), Romanian language required; dual citizenship allowed.

POLAND — Moderate, business capital, Active

Business route: register a Polish sp. z o.o. or similar. To renew residence past the first year, the company must satisfy ONE of: annual income ≥ 12× the average regional monthly wage (approximately USD 25,000/year in the preceding year), OR employ 2 locals full-time for 12 months, OR present credible development plans (per official Ministry of Interior / voivodeship sources). A genuinely revenue-producing company can pass on income alone — no local employees strictly required. However, this is an active-operating-business route: the company must actually generate revenue or hire staff, so a paper/holding company won't clear renewal. Personal income tax progressive up to 32% (plus 4% solidarity levy on high earners); 19% flat option available for some business income.

PR: Available after 5 years of continuous legal residence (long-term/EU permit). Citizenship: Available after 10 years of continuous residence, with B1 Polish language required. Dual citizenship allowed.

CZECHIA — Moderate, $10k, Active

Softest active-business route in the EU set. Two options: trade license (živno / živnostenský list) — the classic Czech freelance/sole-trader route, or s.r.o. (LLC), which has no minimum capital, no company income threshold, and no employee requirement. Applicant must show approximately USD 10,000 in personal funds. First permit typically 2 years, renewals require tax-compliant declared income and continued purpose of stay; consulates informally want to see Czech-market clients (not enforced strictly). This makes Czechia the most passive-friendly of the EU business routes — no active revenue thresholds, no payroll obligations. 15% flat personal income tax; 21% corporate; 60% residential rental reduction options available.

PR: Available after 5 years of continuous legal residence. Citizenship: Available after 10 years of continuous residence, with B1 Czech language required. Czechia formally requires renunciation of prior citizenship at the ceremony (the goal), though dual-citizenship exceptions exist by treaty for some countries; not with the US.

EUROPE — SOUTHERN & BALKANS

ITALY — Easy, €32k/yr income, Passive

Show ~€32,000/year passive income (~€38k for a couple); registered lease or property required. No working.

PR: Available after 5 years (EU long-term residence, B1-ish Italian required). Citizenship: Available after 10 years of residence with B1 Italian; dual citizenship allowed.

GREECE (FIP) — Easy, €2k/mo income, Passive

Show steady passive income of at least €2,000/month (+20% spouse, +15% per child), or equivalent savings (~€48k for two years).

PR: Available after 5 years of residence (long-term resident status). Citizenship: Available after 7 years of genuine residence with B1 Greek and a culture test; dual citizenship allowed.

GREECE (Golden Visa) — Easy, €250k+, Passive

Current tiers (Law 5100/2024, transition windows closed): €800,000 in Attica (all Athens), Thessaloniki, Mykonos, Santorini, and islands >3,100 population; €400,000 everywhere else; €250,000 ONLY for commercial-to-residential conversions or listed-building restorations — anywhere. Also a €250,000 startup route via Elevate Greece companies (Law 5162/2024). Airbnb-style rental banned (€50,000 fine + revocation). 5-year renewable permit, no stay requirement.

Quantified hold outcomes (see Flip-Hold-Analysis.md for full math):

  • 5-year GV cycle (€250k conversion route): +€75k net all-in ($88k USD) — €41k from 5.5% CAGR appreciation on €250k, plus €34k net long-term rental income (Airbnb banned but long-term letting yields 4-5% gross).
  • 7-year hold to actual PR (requires physical residence + B1 Greek): +€100k net all-in ($118k USD).

Math anchors: Athens center €2,439/m² (BoG Q3 2025), 2024/2025/Q1 2026 appreciation 8.8%/7.8%/5.6% (slowing to ~5-6% forecast), 7-8% total in-costs, 5% exit costs. Conversion tier requires legal change-of-use documentation.

Greece is the strongest positive-EV path on this document. You gain financially AND get EU residence. Not a loss vehicle.

PR: NOT via the golden visa — it's a 5-year renewable residence permit tied to holding the qualifying asset, not permanent residence. Formal long-term/EU-permanent-resident status still requires 5 years of actual physical residence. Citizenship: Available after 7 years of GENUINE residence (tax residency, presence) + B1 Greek — holding the visa without living there earns you nothing toward citizenship.

MALTA — Instant, €150k+ all-in, Passive

MPRP: property purchase or rental + government contribution + donation (all-in realistically ~€150,000–€180,000+ via the rental option) — requires proof of ~€500k in assets (€150k liquid).

PR: Available and IMMEDIATE — the MPRP grants permanent residency outright. Citizenship: Not realistically available — Malta's paid citizenship scheme was struck down by the EU Court of Justice in April 2025, and ordinary naturalization is discretionary with no fixed entitlement timeline.

CYPRUS — Instant, €300k, Passive

€300,000 (+VAT) in NEW real estate (funds/shares options too), plus ~€50k secure annual foreign income, grants expedited status within months. Visit once every 2 years. EU but not yet Schengen.

Quantified 5-year hold outcome (see Flip-Hold-Analysis.md for full math):

  • Investor (19% VAT): –€25k to +€3k net all-in. VAT hit is €57k on €300k, dominates the loss. Rental income of €40k (long-term letting at 4.5% gross, 60% net) closes most of the gap.
  • Primary resident (5% VAT): +€6k to +€30k net all-in. VAT savings of €42k make this modestly positive; assumes you actually live there and forgo rental income.

Math anchors: New-build premium ~20% over resale in Limassol (Investropa/Landbank Analytics Jan 2026); 5.5% CAGR base case (62% nominal appreciation over 10 years); 5% exit costs; long-term rental 4-5% gross. Selling terminates PR — no immediate flip strategy possible.

Liquidity caveat (property version): the qualifying purchase must be NEW property, and future PR-seeking foreigners must also buy NEW — so they cannot buy your resale. You exit into the ordinary Cypriot secondary market at ~20% below new-build (about €60-80k gap on €300k), but appreciation over 5 years typically closes most of that. The funds/shares alternatives avoid the VAT hit entirely — prefer them if eligible.

PR: Available and IMMEDIATE — this program grants permanent residency itself within a few months. Citizenship: Available after ~7 of the last 10 years of actual residence (recent reforms allow as little as 4–5 years with Greek language for qualifying applicants); dual citizenship allowed.

SERBIA — Easy, no minimum, Passive

Property of ANY value → temporary residence (up to 1–3 years under the 2024 unified rules; renewable while you own it; expect a ~183-day/year presence expectation for property-based renewals). Or register a company — no minimum capital, no employees, no trading requirement (renewals want ~€5,000 into share capital or taxes). Flat 10–20% personal tax. Cheapest formal property-residency in Europe.

PR: Available after 3 years of continuous residence (absences up to 10 months total allowed). Citizenship: Available unusually fast — you can apply as soon as you hold PR (~3–4 years total); ordinary naturalization expects renunciation of prior citizenship (the goal). Serbian-language and residency-proof are the real gates.

MONTENEGRO — Easy, no minimum, Passive

Property of ANY value has long granted a 1-year renewable temporary permit (plus ~€3,650 bank balance and insurance) — but property-based permits can be revoked if you're out >90 days; the company-director route (~€5,000/year in taxes+contributions) is the flexible alternative. January 2026 added a codified €150,000 property investment pathway. EU accession target 2028 — cheap call option on future EU status.

PR: Available after 5 years of continuous temporary residence. Citizenship: Available after 10 years, with renunciation required (the goal). Montenegrin/Serbian language is the real barrier.

ALBANIA — Easy, no minimum, Passive

Unique Permit with NO formal minimum — residency via business formation, property purchase, or securities of essentially any value (guidance: ≥20 m² dwelling per person; Durrës beach studios from ~€36k). Also a pensioner/retiree permit on passive income. Tax 0/13/23%, 8% dividends. Americans get a full year visa-free anyway.

PR: Available after 5 years of continuous legal residence. Citizenship: Available after ~7 years of residence with basic Albanian — and DUAL CITIZENSHIP IS ALLOWED, which sets Albania apart from Serbia and Montenegro.

NORTH MACEDONIA — Moderate, €5k, Active

Company formation route: register a DOO (LLC, 2–50 founders, EUR 5,000 minimum share capital, ~USD 5,400 — payable in cash or in-kind, and may be paid within one year of registration) or a DOOEL (single-founder LLC, same EUR 5,000 minimum). There is also a PDOO (simplified LLC) that can be founded with just EUR 1, but it must set aside 25% of annual profit into a mandatory reserve until that reserve reaches standard share-capital level. As the company director you may apply for a temporary residence permit on the basis of your role — valid 1 year, renewable annually (up to 3 years or unlimited period in some cases based on net worth and business plan). Requires a detailed business plan showing investment amount, projected local jobs, and economic benefits; proof of financial means; health insurance; clean criminal record. Registration is fast: Central Registry decisions typically in 5 business days, sometimes 24–48 hours. Personal income tax and corporate tax both flat 10%; VAT standard 18%.

PR: Available after 5 years of continuous temporary residence (no absences exceeding 6 months continuous or 10 months discontinuous). Citizenship: Available after 8 years of continuous residence, with Macedonian-language and history/culture exams. Renunciation generally required. Language is the real gate.

BOSNIA & HERZEGOVINA — Easy, no minimum, Passive

Two easy routes. Property route: ownership of any residential or commercial property, no minimum value, grants basis for a 1-year renewable temporary residence permit (Sarajevo apartments start around EUR 60,000–150,000; Mostar 1-bed apartments from ~EUR 80,000; provincial towns much less). Company route: register a d.o.o. (LLC) with EUR 500 minimum capital (roughly USD 540); as founder/director you can apply for temporary residence on the business basis, 1 year renewable. Bosnia is unique in the region for 0% dividend withholding tax on top of a 10% flat corporate tax — meaning dividends can be extracted post-corporate-tax with no additional layer. 10% flat personal income tax.

PR: Available after 5 years of continuous lawful temporary residence (with allowances for total absences up to 10 months, or single absence up to 6 months). Citizenship: Available after 8 years of continuous residence; Bosnia does not recognize dual citizenship except by bilateral treaty (US has none) — so renunciation required (the goal). Language competency and integration expectations apply.

TURKEY — Easy, $200k, Passive

Citizenship: property worth $400,000 held 3 years → citizenship for you and family. For plain residency: since 2023 a property-based short-term residence permit generally requires a $200,000+ property, and many 'closed' districts (>20–25% foreigner ratio) refuse new registrations — check the district first.

Quantified 3-year hold outcome on the $400k CBI route (see Flip-Hold-Analysis.md for full math): Base case (7% USD annual appreciation, 6% gross yield): +$59k net all-in over 3 years, plus the passport. Optimistic (10% appreciation, 7% yield): +$112k. Pessimistic (3% appreciation, 5% yield): ~break-even. Positive-EV in most scenarios. Math anchors: central Istanbul ~$2,000/m² (TCMB HPI), 4% transfer tax + 7-12% overhead ($28-48k), 25% YoY USD appreciation Q1 2026 with sustainable 5-10% base case (istanbulrealestate.net, binaainvestment.com).

MANDATORY 3-YEAR HOLD is annotated on the title deed since the 2025 update — cannot flip within window without government pre-approval. Citizenship survives after 3 years even if you then sell.

Currency risk caveat: structure exit through hard-currency-denominated resale (branded residences, premium new-builds are effectively USD-pegged). Ordinary secondary market transacts in lira and exposes you to lira depreciation on exit.

PR: Available after 8 years of uninterrupted residence on the standard track (the $200k property permit is renewable in the meantime). Citizenship: Available IMMEDIATELY via the $400,000 property purchase (3-year hold, then free to sell while retaining citizenship forever) — Turkey is the region's flagship citizenship-by-investment and allows dual citizenship; otherwise 5 years of residence with heavy presence requirements.

CAUCASUS

GEORGIA — Easy, $300k, Passive

$300k investment route. Property or business above $300,000 → 5-year Investment Residence Permit. Business version requires turnover of $50k/$100k/$120k in years 1/2/3–5.

Liquidity note (property version): Tbilisi runs ~$1,200–1,800/m², so a typical decent apartment is $80–150k — a single $300k property is ~2x the normal local transaction. Mitigation: the threshold can be met with MULTIPLE units (e.g. three $100k apartments), which sell individually to ordinary local buyers with no visa-premium markup. Structure it that way and the liquidity trap disappears.

PR: Available — the $300k route converts to PERMANENT residency after 5 years of holding the asset. Citizenship: Available after 10 years of continuous legal residence plus exams in Georgian language, history, and law; dual citizenship needs a discretionary presidential exception.

Territorial taxation (foreign income untaxed), 5% tax on residential rental income, cheap living. Most nationalities get 365-day visa-free entry, so you can live there a year with no permit at all.

ARMENIA — Instant, ~$6k donation, Passive

No minimum investment anywhere in the law. Register a sole proprietorship or one-person company (no capital requirement, ~2–3 days) and genuine activity qualifies you for residency. Property alone does NOT qualify — but renting it out and paying the 5% gross rental tax converts it into qualifying business activity. A marketed ~$6,000 social-enterprise donation route grants immediate permanent residency. No stay requirement; ~$270 fee; one of the cheapest legitimate programs on Earth.

PR: Available — permanent residency can be obtained immediately via the ~$6,000 donation route, or after holding temporary residency via the business route. Citizenship: Available after just 3 years of holding residency (no heavy physical-presence enforcement in practice), passing a constitution test; DUAL CITIZENSHIP IS ALLOWED — the fastest realistic second passport in this document.

AZERBAIJAN — Easy, $118k, Passive

PR-level route: real estate, term bank deposit, or state/state-majority securities worth AZN 200,000 (~$118,000) — hold at this level through 2 years of temporary residence, then convert to permanent residence. The property should be owner-occupied — renting it out can void the basis. $118k is within the normal range for a decent Baku apartment, so the resale market is ordinary local buyers — no visa-premium liquidity trap.

PR: Available — permanent residence requires holding the qualifying asset at the higher AZN 200,000 (~$118,000) level plus 2 years of temporary residence first. Citizenship: Available after 5 years of permanent residence with an Azerbaijani language test; Azerbaijan bans dual citizenship (fine — that forces the renunciation, which is the goal).

CENTRAL ASIA

UZBEKISTAN — Instant, $100k, Passive

Real estate route (the only PR route). Property → PERMANENT residence card: $300,000 Tashkent/region; $200,000 Samarkand, Bukhara, Namangan, Andijan, Fergana, Khorezm; $100,000 Karakalpakstan and other regions. Restricted to a register of ~111 nationalities (US confirmed on the list per multiple 2025-2026 sources). Must be NEW construction (< 3 years since commissioning), single unit only per notary practice.

Quantified loss estimates (see Flip-Hold-Analysis.md for full math + sources):

  • $100k regional immediate flip (if legal): –$60–72k loss. Sell to local at ~$30–42k. Still $141–153k better than Saudi's $213k sunk fee.
  • $100k regional 5-yr hold: –$19k gross, +$10k net with rental income (roughly break-even).
  • $300k Tashkent immediate flip (if legal): –$115k loss (sell as secondary at ~$195k). Still $98k better than Saudi.
  • $300k Tashkent 5-yr hold: –$21k gross, +$22k net with rental income (modestly positive).

Math anchors: Tashkent premium new-build $2,100–2,350/m² vs secondary $1,300–1,600/m² in same districts (CERR data via kun.uz Apr 2026, internationalinvestment.biz May 2026). New-build premium ~30–45%. Regional Navoi at $636/m² is proxy for Karakalpakstan-tier pricing.

CRITICAL LEGAL OPEN QUESTIONS — verify with an Uzbek immigration lawyer before relying on this route:

  1. Does PR survive sale of the qualifying property? Article 11 of the Uzbek Law on Legal Status of Foreign Citizens lists ID-card cancellation grounds — the accessible ones focus on absence from Uzbekistan, NOT on selling qualifying property. But the permit "is done up to 5 times to the period established, after which its change is done" — periodic reissuance is a review point.
  2. Tashkent alienation restriction: Per Azizov & Partners memo (Jun 2026), sale of Tashkent property by a foreign PR holder is permitted only if the buyer has permanent registration in Tashkent AND 3+ years residence there — a massive buyer-pool restriction. The Oct 2025 propiska abolition MAY have lifted this, but no confirmation surfaced. Regional cities do not have this restriction (Karakalpakstan/other tier is safer on this dimension).
  3. Rental of qualifying property: Azerbaijan explicitly voids the visa basis if the property is rented out; Uzbek rules on this not clearly surfaced in searches. Verify before assuming rental income is available during the hold period.

Budget $200–400 for a Tashkent immigration lawyer consultation covering all three questions before committing. Answers determine whether Uzbekistan is the best PR-per-dollar on this document (flip works, PR survives, rent OK) or worse than expected (holding required, thin resale, no rent).

PR: Available — the real-estate route grants an indefinite permanent residence card directly (from $100k in the regions), subject to the caveats above about periodic reissuance and open legal questions. Citizenship: Nominally available after 5 years of residence; Uzbekistan bans dual citizenship (fine), but adult naturalization grants are rare in practice — treat as a PR play, not a passport play.

KYRGYZSTAN — Moderate, $114k, Active

Formal investment route: Investment Visa Category "I" requires 10 million KGS (~USD 114,000) for a 5-year visa or 20 million KGS (~USD 229,000) for a 10-year visa, deployed into ICT, industry, agriculture, banking, energy, education, medicine, or construction/engineering per the current Kyrgyz e-Visa page. Slightly over your ~$60k cap — verify current thresholds directly with the State Migration Service since the older Law on External Migration also referenced $20k/1yr, $200k/3yr, and $500k/5yr tiers that may still exist in parallel. Also available: 60-day visa-free entry for US citizens (extendable to 120 days at OVIR in Bishkek); business/self-employment residence via company registration under the general framework (KG LLC minimum capital is nominal ~1 som). Business environment concerns: Freedom House flagged the 2024 Russian-style "foreign representatives" NGO law and the August 2025 media law requiring registration and 35% foreign-ownership cap; World Bank notes growing state-owned enterprise presence and weak business environment. Personal income tax 10% flat; foreign-source income taxed only if remitted (verify).

MIDDLE EAST

JORDAN — Easy, $282k, Passive

Real Estate PR. Buy property valued at JOD 200,000 (~$282,000 USD), provided it is held without being sold or mortgaged; gets you permanent or long-term 5-year residency.

Wealth PR. Local bank deposit of JOD 300,000 (~$423,000 USD) for 3 years, investing JOD 150,000 (~$211,000 USD) into a local company, or buying JOD 350,000 (~$493,000 USD) in Treasury bonds.

Quantified 5-year hold-then-sell outcome (see Flip-Hold-Analysis.md for full math): Base case (flat prices, 7% gross yield): break-even ~-$500. Optimistic (2% appreciation, 9% yield): +$64k. Pessimistic (5% depreciation, 6% yield): -$46k. The entire trade lives on rental yield — without rent, you lose $35-95k. With normal rent, roughly break-even. Note: Amman pricing data is 2018-vintage (Global Property Guide) — verify current per-m² pricing with an Amman broker before committing.

Material caveats on the property route:

  • Property must be NEW — the visa program does not accept resale/secondary-market properties.
  • Properties in the qualifying tier are high-end and depreciate roughly 10–20% over a 10-year hold, so on a $282k entry you should expect $30–55k of value erosion before you exit.
  • Resale liquidity is genuinely poor. The natural buyer pool is other foreigners seeking the same visa — but they must buy NEW property to qualify, so they cannot buy yours. You're selling into the ordinary Jordanian resale market, which is thin at that price point.
  • Selling terminates PR. So the loss math is only relevant if you're using Jordan as a stepping stone (to $750k+ citizenship) or as an option-value hold you'll abandon. If Jordan is home, this doesn't apply.

PR: Available — holding the qualifying asset grants residency indefinitely. Note the material caveats below on property choice, depreciation, and resale liquidity. Citizenship: Realistically available only via the direct investment track — $750,000+ USD in qualifying financial investment leads to full Jordanian citizenship for you and family; ordinary naturalization otherwise requires ~15 years and is discretionary and rare.

Amazing educational system, the highest inbound medical tourism in the Gulf because of its outstanding healthcare services quality, huge and rapidly growing culture around riding dirt bikes offroad, and mountains everywhere with moderate and even cold climate. Ad valorem tax on the truck of around 56% of value to import; they will use Hagerty, Classic.com or Hemmings to establish a fair global market value based on what others like it are selling for at auctions. The truck is valued based off its originality, structural integrity, and condition grade, not the stereo system or other modifications. Hagerty's has it valued around 10–15k.

SAUDI ARABIA — Instant, $213k, Passive

$1.1M USD property investment gets you immediate Premium Residency. A $213,000 (SAR 800,000) one-time fee also gives you immediate Premium Residency.

PR: Available and immediate — the SAR 800,000 (~$213k) one-time-fee Premium Residency is itself PERMANENT (the annual-fee version is the renewable one). This is the cheapest instant-PR purchase in the Gulf. Citizenship: Effectively unavailable — Saudi naturalization is discretionary, extremely rare, and Premium Residency confers no path to it.
